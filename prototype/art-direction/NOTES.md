# Original-IP art-direction prototype notes

## Question

What original visual language gives the four Classes, opponents, Stage
backdrops, and effects a bright chibi fantasy appeal while remaining
recognizably distinct from Ragnarok Online and readable at the compact
Battlefield's native scale?

## Directions presented

- **A — Sunsteel Caravan:** heraldic traveling-company fantasy with heavy navy
  contours, coral/teal/gold uniforms, pennant silhouettes, warm road backdrops,
  seed-creature opponents, and ribbon-like weapon effects.
- **B — Moonberry Guild:** storybook night-garden fantasy with plum contours,
  mint/berry/cream colors, leaf and stitched silhouettes, orchard backdrops,
  mushroom opponents, and petal/halo magic.
- **C — Skyglass Company:** prismatic expedition fantasy with indigo contours,
  cyan/amber/white uniforms, crystalline geometry, cloud-shelf backdrops,
  kite-shaped opponents, and precise rune effects.

All directions use the same exact 32×48-at-1× Character canvas in the selected
480×112 Battle Tile, show three Party Members against a five-opponent stress
case, keep body animation separate from effects, and include fixed canonical
silhouettes for all four Classes.

## Verdict

The user found both Sunsteel Caravan and Moonberry Guild acceptable and
preferred **Moonberry Guild**. Moonberry is therefore the canonical art
direction for the vertical slice: storybook night-garden fantasy, plum contour
lines, mint/berry/cream color families, plump leaf and stitched silhouettes,
orchard-like Stage backdrops, whimsical botanical opponents, and petal/halo
Ability effects.

Sunsteel does not remain a second direction. Its bold-contour and warm-contrast
discipline is retained only as a legibility guardrail when Moonberry assets are
reviewed at 32×48 and 1×. Skyglass and its crystalline magitech language are
rejected for this slice. The comparison switcher and losing visual variants
were removed from the runnable prototype.
