# Define Equipment, Drops, and storage

Type: grilling
Status: resolved
Blocked by: 05, 06

## Question

What Equipment slots, Class restrictions, item identities, randomized properties, rarity structure, Drop rules, storage model, comparison flow, and disposal rules create a satisfying idle reward loop at vertical-slice scale?

## Answer

- Each Character has three Equipment Slots in the vertical slice: Weapon, Armor, and Charm. The full game is expected to expand to six slots, but the additional slots are not specified here. Equipment uses icons and statistics only and never changes a Character's fixed combat appearance.
- Weapons are Class-specific: Knight blade, Wizard focus, Priest relic, and Hunter bow. Armor Equipment and Charms are usable by every Class. The Reserve equips normally. Moving Equipment is free, but one piece can belong to only one Character's Equipment Loadout at a time.
- Every Equipment Drop has an authored Equipment Base, an Item Level equal to its source Stage (1-3), a Rarity, and randomized Affixes. The Base provides its slot, icon, name stem, restriction, and one guaranteed statistic; Rarity controls the Affix count.
- Rarities are Common (zero Affixes), Uncommon (one), Rare (two), and Epic (three). The same Affix cannot repeat on one piece. Epic Equipment combines ordinary statistics and has no unique procs or bespoke effects in the slice.
- Affixes are flat Physical, percentage Physical Power, flat Elemental, percentage Elemental Power, flat Max Health, percentage Max Health, flat Armor, and flat Elemental Resistance. Weapons roll their Class-aligned offensive pair plus all four defensive Affixes; Armor Equipment rolls only the defensive four; Charms roll all eight. Critical, timing, proc, and individual-Element Affixes are outside the slice.
- Each Equipment Tier authors six Equipment Bases: one blade, focus, relic, bow, universal Armor Equipment, and universal Charm. Tier I covers Item Levels 1-2 from Stages 1-2; Tier II begins at Item Level 3 from the repeatable Stage 3 frontier. The Tier changes name stems, icons, guaranteed-stat scale, and Affix range, so the slice needs 12 Base icons. Rarity remains independent, allowing an exceptional Tier I piece to outperform a weak Tier II piece.
- Guaranteed Base statistics change only at a Tier boundary, not at each Item Level. Tier I blade/bow gives +2 Physical, focus/relic +2 Elemental, Armor Equipment +4 Armor, and Charm +8 Max Health. Tier II gives +5 Physical, +5 Elemental, +9 Armor, and +18 Max Health respectively.
- Affix rolls are inclusive whole numbers and change only by Tier. Tier I/Tier II bands are: flat Physical or Elemental `+1-2 / +3-5`; Physical or Elemental Power `+4-8% / +8-14%`; flat Max Health `+6-12 / +14-24`; Max Health `+4-8% / +8-14%`; Armor or Elemental Resistance `+3-6 / +7-12`. Flat and percentage Affixes for the same statistic may coexist because they are distinct Affixes.
- Clearing each ordinary Wave awards one Equipment Drop; defeating each Boss awards two. A complete Stage awards four and a clean first pass through all three Stages awards 12. Rewards enter storage at encounter completion and are retained through later Party Defeat or abandonment. There is no first-clear bonus. Item Level equals the source Stage.
- Drop type selection first rolls Weapon, Armor, or Charm uniformly. A Weapon result then chooses one of the four Classes uniformly. The source Tier selects the appropriate Equipment Base before Rarity and Affixes are rolled. This gives each equipped slot category one-third of all Drops rather than overweighting the four Weapon Bases.
- Rarity odds are Stage 1 `55% Common / 35% Uncommon / 9% Rare / 1% Epic`; Stage 2 `40/40/17/3`; Stage 3 `25/45/24/6`. For each Boss's two Drops, the second has an Uncommon floor: a Common result becomes Uncommon without rerolling the rest of the piece.
- Loot uses a persisted RNG stream separate from deterministic combat. Each Equipment piece is fully rolled and saved when awarded, so reload or abandonment cannot reroll it. Repeated Attempts vary, Offline Progress consumes the same stream, and fixed seeds remain available for reproducible tests without being player-visible.
- All owned Equipment lives in one shared, capacity-free Armory, including equipped pieces. An equipped piece carries its exclusive Character-and-slot assignment rather than moving to another collection. Views may filter assigned and available pieces. The slice has no per-Character bags, capacity upgrades, overflow, or inventory-full interruption.
- Comparison starts from a Character's Equipment Slot and shows compatible candidates only. It presents the selected and equipped pieces side-by-side, individual statistic deltas, and resulting raw Ability Damage/Healing changes, while consolidated Power totals remain hidden. There is no aggregate score or auto-equip. Moving a piece from another Character requires confirmation and leaves that Character's slot empty. Equipment edits during a Stage Attempt apply to the next Attempt.
- Equipment may be manually Locked. Equipped and Locked pieces cannot be discarded. The Armory supports multi-select and permanent bulk Discard with confirmation; Rare and Epic selections are explicitly listed in that confirmation. The slice has no automatic disposal, loot filter, selling, salvage currency, crafting, upgrading, or Affix rerolling.
- Drops never pause Automatic Combat or open a modal. A compact icon-and-Rarity toast appears over the Battle Tile and fades; two Boss Drops appear sequentially. New and Offline Progress pieces are marked Unseen. Opening a piece clears the marker. The Armory defaults to Unseen first and then newest first; Offline Drops also appear in the reopening summary.
- Affix types are selected uniformly without replacement from the eligible slot pool, and each whole-number value is selected uniformly from its inclusive Tier range. Offensive and defensive Affixes have no hidden weighting. A Boss Rarity floor changes Affix count only, not rolled values.
- Armory filters cover Equipment Slot, Weapon Class, Equipment Tier, Rarity, assigned/available, Locked, and Unseen. Sorts are newest, Rarity, Tier, and name. Combinable filters persist until cleared; the default remains Unseen first then newest. The slice has no text search because it contains only 12 Equipment Bases.
- Every Character begins with all three Equipment Slots empty. Class base statistics support initial combat, so the first guaranteed Wave Drop begins the Equipment progression.
- Item Level is not a Character Level requirement. Equipment does not bind, form sets, require minimum statistics, or require a specific Stage clear. Any slot- and Class-compatible owned piece may be assigned, with its combat effects beginning on the next Stage Attempt.
- Each of the 12 Equipment Bases has one authored original-IP display name and icon. Affixes do not generate name prefixes or suffixes. Rarity color, Tier badge, Item Level, guaranteed statistic, and Affix list distinguish duplicate Bases; each Drop retains its own internal unique identity.
